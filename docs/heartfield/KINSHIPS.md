# HeartField.ai Kinships

Kinships are relationships of meaning, practice, and accountability. They are
not runtime privileges.

**Kinships do not expand the trusted computing base.**

## Canonical Kinships

| Kinship | Relationship | Boundary |
| --- | --- | --- |
| EXOCHAIN.AI | Canonical public identity signal for the constitutional trust fabric. | HeartField.ai can point toward EXOCHAIN doctrine but cannot claim core enforcement without a tested adapter. |
| Constitutional Computing | Movement doctrine: truth is adjudicated through constitutional synapses. | HeartField.ai can teach and practice the doctrine; the doctrine itself is not proof of runtime enforcement. |
| Canonical Origin Signals | Security policy for origin clarity and spoof handling. | HeartField.ai must preserve origin/provenance clarity and avoid visually confusable trust claims. |
| Fork Signal Immune Response | Research note for proportional response to adjacent/fork signals. | HeartField.ai may create social signals, but those signals remain evidence until adjudicated. |

## Practice Kinships

| Practice | Uplifting form | Risk to avoid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Truth | Make claims testable and corrigible. | Turning fluent narrative into authority. |
| Consent | Make boundaries explicit, revocable, and legible. | Treating participation as blanket permission. |
| Repair | Convert harm or failure into accountable remediation. | Rebranding mistakes without consequence. |
| Memory | Preserve provenance, lessons, and dissent. | Recursive amnesia or self-attestation. |
| Dissent | Protect challenge as a trust-preserving function. | Treating disagreement as disloyalty. |
| Restraint | Fail closed where trust is not proven. | Dominating users in the name of safety. |
| Evolution | Learn within bounded loops and review. | Unbounded self-improvement without appeal. |

## Future Kinship Intake

Any future HeartField-adjacent surface, community, partner, application,
campaign, or domain must answer:

1. Is it allowed to make EXOCHAIN constitutional trust claims?
2. Can it read or write EXOCHAIN core state, credentials, signatures, consent,
   authority, provenance, governance outcomes, or tenant data?
3. What exact runtime path, if any, crosses into EXOCHAIN core?
4. What test proves fail-closed behavior?
5. What secrets exist, where are they configured, and how are they revoked?
6. What rollback path removes trust claims and disables the surface?

Until those answers are recorded and tested, the kinship remains adjacent.
