## 1. OVERVIEW & NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
**Product Vision**  
A constitutional governance protocol where every board resolution, committee decision, delegation, and policy is a sovereign, cryptographically verifiable object. Fiduciary defense is a natural byproduct. The platform governs its own development (recursive self-modification) via the same rules it enforces on customers.

**Core Axioms (Non-Negotiable)**  
1. Authority is held in trust, never owned.  
2. Decisions are first-class sovereign objects (storable, diffable, transferable, auditable, contestable).  
3. Trust accumulation > speed.  
4. Constitutional constraints are machine-readable and enforced at runtime.  
5. Authority without cryptographic provenance is void.

**Target Scale & Performance**  
- 10K tenants, 1M decisions/day, 50-year immutable history  
- P99 latency <200ms for chain verification  
- 99.999% availability (multi-region active-active)  
- Post-quantum ready from day 1  
- WCAG 2.2 AA + neurodiversity compliant

**Tech Stack Constraints (for coding)**  
- Backend: Rust/Go or TypeScript (your choice) with Merkle-DAG + Raft/CRDT  
- Crypto: Ed25519 → hybrid Kyber/ML-DSA post-quantum  
- Storage: S3-compatible cold tier + hot LRU cache  
- Formal verification: TLA+ for all invariants  
- AI layer: zkML provenance + mandatory human gates

## 2. GOVERNANCE REQUIREMENTS (GOV-xxx)

**GOV-001: Machine-Readable Constitutional Framework** [P0]  
The system SHALL maintain a signed, versioned, machine-readable constitutional corpus (JSON/YAML schema) per tenant containing charter, bylaws, policies, delegation matrices, and jurisdiction rules.  
Acceptance Criteria:  
- Real-time constraint evaluation (<2s)  
- Every Decision Object stores hash of active constitution version  
- Dry-run amendment simulator  
Failure Mode: $15M contract approved with simple majority when bylaws required 2/3 → blocked & logged.

**GOV-002: Constitutional Versioning with Temporal Binding** [P0]  
Every Decision Object MUST be bound to the exact constitutional version(s) in force at creation. Retroactive amendments prohibited by default.  
Acceptance Criteria:  
- Semantic versioning + immutable history  
- Query: "Under which version was Decision X compliant?" with cryptographic proof  
- Amendments themselves are Decision Objects.

**GOV-003: Delegated Authority Matrix** [P0]  
Real-time matrix mapping every actor (human/AI) to scoped, time-bound, revocable authorities with full chain provenance.  
Acceptance Criteria:  
- Signed delegation records (JSON + Ed25519)  
- Auto-expiry enforcement  
- Full chain verification <2s (up to 5 levels)  
- Sub-delegation only if explicitly permitted and scope-capped.

**GOV-004: Standing Authority Sunset & Renewal** [P1]  
All standing authorities expire (max 12 months default) and require affirmative renewal via new Decision Object.  
Acceptance Criteria:  
- 90/60/30/14/7-day notifications  
- Expired → blocked immediately  
- Sunset calendar dashboard.

**GOV-005: Authority Chain Verification on Every State Change** [P0]  
Every action requires synchronous cryptographic chain verification from constitutional source to actor.  
Acceptance Criteria:  
- <2s verification  
- Broken chain → CHAIN_BREAK rejection with diagnostic  
- Offline portable verifier tool.

**GOV-006: Constitutional Conflict Resolution Hierarchy** [P1]  
Deterministic hierarchy (Articles > Bylaws > Resolutions > Charters > Policies) with human escalation for conflicts.  
Acceptance Criteria:  
- Auto-block + route to GC/Board  
- Conflict Register with audit trail.

**GOV-007: Human Oversight Gates for AI-Assisted Decisions** [P0]  
AI agents are delegated-only; cannot hold original authority or satisfy HUMAN_GATE_REQUIRED classes.  
Acceptance Criteria:  
- AI signatures cryptographically distinct  
- HUMAN_GATE_REQUIRED list modifiable only via constitutional amendment  
- Max AI delegation 90 days.

**GOV-008: Structured Contestation & Reversal Protocol** [P0]  
Every Decision Object supports formal contestation by authorized stakeholders.  
Acceptance Criteria:  
- Creates linked Challenge Object  
- CONTESTED status pauses execution (except emergencies)  
- Resolution is itself a Decision Object; reversal creates immutable REVERSAL link.

**GOV-009: Emergency Action Protocol with Retrospective Ratification** [P0]  
Expedited actions under strict EMERGENCY_AUTHORITY with mandatory ratification within 7 days.  
Acceptance Criteria:  
- Limited scope + monetary caps  
- Auto-creates RATIFICATION_REQUIRED follow-up  
- Frequency tracking + governance review if >3/quarter.

**GOV-010: Quorum Failure & Graceful Degradation** [P1]  
Defined degradation protocols when quorum impossible.  
Acceptance Criteria:  
- Auto-detect → DEGRADED_GOVERNANCE status  
- Restricted actions + mandatory later ratification.

**GOV-011: Succession & Continuity Protocols** [P1]  
Pre-defined, automatically activated succession chains for all key roles.  
Acceptance Criteria:  
- At least 2 successors per role  
- Triggers: declaration, unresponsiveness, or designated activator.

**GOV-012: Accountability Mechanisms (Censure, Suspension, Revocation, Recall)** [P1]  
Formal accountability actions as Decision Objects.  
Acceptance Criteria:  
- Immediate suspension enforcement (<60s)  
- Due process timelines enforced by system.

**GOV-013: Recursive Self-Governance & Safe Meta-Modification** [P0]  
All platform evolution (sprints, model upgrades, constitutional changes) MUST be governed as Decision Objects under the same rules.  
Acceptance Criteria:
