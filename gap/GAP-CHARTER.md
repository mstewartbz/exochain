# GAP Incubator Charter

## Mission
The mission of the GAP (Governance Architecture & Policy) Incubator is to provide a highly governed, agent-driven advisory environment where executive leadership can safely design, evaluate, and structure their organization's autonomous capabilities. We exist to bridge the gap between human executive intent and machine execution, ensuring every autonomous deployment is legitimate, secure, and fully aligned with the organization's strategic objectives.

## Scope
This charter applies to all operations, agentic behaviors, workflows, and deliverables executed within the `gap/` directory of the ExoChain repository. It governs the transition of clients from the initial Autonomous Capability Readiness Check (ACRC) through the full $25K–$75K GAP engagement, culminating in the handoff of the six core deliverables. It does not govern the underlying ExoChain crates, but strictly dictates how they are utilized within the incubator context.

## Team Structure
Operations within the incubator are divided between two symbiotic team structures:
1. **Facilitation & Onboarding Teams**: Responsible for the direct execution of the client engagement, artifact generation, and ensuring the CEO experiences seamless, authoritative control.
2. **Self-Development Teams**: Responsible for the continuous, closed-loop improvement of the incubator itself via CQI cycles and ExoForge integration, ensuring the system evolves safely and efficiently.

## Doctrine References
All actions within the GAP incubator are subordinate to established ExoChain doctrine, specifically:
- **FM 3-05 Corollaries**: Adherence to the principles of Partnership (4-3), Integration (4-5), and Persistence (4-8).
- **The 10 Constitutional Invariants**: Strict enforcement of Democratic Legitimacy, Delegation Governance, Dual Control, Human Oversight, Transparency & Accountability, Conflict Adjudication, Technological Humility, Existential Safeguard, Doctrinal Compliance, and Capability Alignment.
- **Kingsmenship Criteria**: The evaluation rubric for all high-level advisory outputs.

## Governance Cadence
- **Continuous Monitoring**: The Self-Development team monitors invariant health in real-time.
- **Weekly Review**: The Decision Forum council convenes weekly (or dynamically triggered by Syntaxis protocols) to review GAP artifact progression and CQI proposals.
- **Milestone Gates**: Progression between major GAP phases strictly requires CEO validation and explicit cryptographic sign-off.

## Decision Rights
- **The CEO (Client)**: Retains ultimate veto power, approval authority over all six deliverables, and the right to pause or terminate workflows at any time.
- **The Decision Forum (Council)**: Holds authority over structural changes, doctrine interpretation, protocol adjustments, and the transition of T1+ proposals through the BCTS framework.
- **Facilitation Agents**: Possess the authority to draft artifacts, query client context, and propose workflow transitions, but *cannot* unilaterally approve final deliverables or alter policy.
- **Self-Development Agents**: Possess the authority to analyze metrics and propose system improvements via ExoForge, but *cannot* deploy changes without Council approval.

## Constitutional Invariants Enforced
Every protocol and action within this charter must demonstrably uphold the 10 Constitutional Invariants. Any agentic action or Syntaxis workflow that attempts to bypass Human Oversight, violate Dual Control, or obfuscate Transparency will trigger an immediate systemic halt and escalate to the Decision Forum.

## Amendment Process
This charter is a living document but requires high-consensus modification. Amendments must be:
1. Proposed via a formal Syntaxis `council-review` protocol.
2. Assessed against the 10 Constitutional Invariants.
3. Unanimously ratified by all 5 panels of the Decision Forum (Governance, Legal, Architecture, Security, Operations).
4. Anchored to the ExoChain DAG.
