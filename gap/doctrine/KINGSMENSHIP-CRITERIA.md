# Kingsmenship Criteria for GAP Engagements

The Kingsmenship Criteria represent the pinnacle standard of advisory quality and operational excellence. Within the `gap/` incubator, these six criteria are used to rigorously evaluate the success of a GAP engagement, the performance of the Facilitation Teams, and the ultimate value delivered to the CEO.

---

### 1. Governance Integrity
- **Criterion:** The fundamental soundness, transparency, and constitutional adherence of the organizational architecture.
- **What it means for GAP:** The resulting *Authority Matrix* and *Policy Gate Library* must be airtight. There can be no ambiguous delegations, conflicting rules, or unchecked autonomous vectors.
- **Measurement:** Syntaxis verification of all policy gates, zero critical invariant violations during the engagement, and successful dry-run simulations of the *Escalation Graph*.
- **Pass/Fail Indicators:** 
  - *Pass:* All policies compile cleanly and the CEO understands the exact boundaries.
  - *Fail:* Ambiguous rules, circular logic in the escalation graph, or "black box" delegations.

### 2. Pipeline Expansion
- **Criterion:** The ability of the engagement to unlock new, scalable operational capacities without compromising control.
- **What it means for GAP:** The *Workflow Control Map* should clearly demonstrate how the organization can safely integrate more autonomous systems (moving from ACRC to MeshCore SaaS). The GAP engagement must remove bottlenecks, not just add bureaucracy.
- **Measurement:** The number of newly secured workflows identified, the reduction in manual review latency (while maintaining safety), and the clarity of the path to SaaS integration.
- **Pass/Fail Indicators:**
  - *Pass:* The CEO can immediately identify three new workflows that can be safely automated under the new governance structure.
  - *Fail:* The governance overhead makes the deployment of new autonomous systems prohibitively slow or complex.

### 3. Reputational Capital
- **Criterion:** The enhancement of the organization’s standing, trust, and perceived reliability among stakeholders.
- **What it means for GAP:** The *Evidence Schema* and *Pilot Outcome Memo* must be of such high quality that they can be shown to board members, regulators, or partners as proof of responsible AI stewardship.
- **Measurement:** Auditability of the DAG trails, clarity of the executive reporting, and the ability of the artifacts to withstand external scrutiny.
- **Pass/Fail Indicators:**
  - *Pass:* Artifacts are presentation-ready for the Board of Directors, proving rigorous oversight.
  - *Fail:* Evidence trails are opaque, or executive summaries read like technical error logs.

### 4. Capital Formation Leverage
- **Criterion:** The strategic positioning of the organization to secure resources, investment, or budget based on operational excellence.
- **What it means for GAP:** By proving that autonomous systems are safely governed, the CEO can justify further investment. The GAP engagement must de-risk the autonomous strategy in the eyes of capital allocators.
- **Measurement:** The financial clarity of the *Pilot Outcome Memo*, specifically highlighting risk mitigation and efficiency gains.
- **Pass/Fail Indicators:**
  - *Pass:* The engagement provides clear data to justify the transition to the MeshCore SaaS tier.
  - *Fail:* The financial impact or risk reduction of the governance architecture remains unquantifiable.

### 5. Safe Intelligence Escalation
- **Criterion:** The ability to dynamically increase the autonomy and intelligence of agents precisely when safe, and aggressively constrain them when uncertain.
- **What it means for GAP:** The *Escalation Graph* must be perfectly tuned. The system must not cry wolf on minor issues, nor fail to escalate critical anomalies.
- **Measurement:** The ratio of true-positive to false-positive escalations during pilot simulations, and the responsiveness of human-in-the-loop triggers.
- **Pass/Fail Indicators:**
  - *Pass:* Escalations route to the right human instantly, with full context.
  - *Fail:* Alert fatigue, or critical decisions bypassed without human notification.

### 6. Multi-Generational Durability
- **Criterion:** The governance architecture must be resilient enough to survive leadership transitions, technology upgrades, and evolving threats.
- **What it means for GAP:** The GAP artifacts cannot be tied to a specific brittle technology. The architecture must be fundamentally rooted in ExoChain's immutable DAG and Syntaxis protocols, ensuring long-term continuity.
- **Measurement:** The successful execution of the CQI (Continuous Quality Improvement) loop by the Self-Development team, proving the system can adapt to changes.
- **Pass/Fail Indicators:**
  - *Pass:* The *Policy Gate Library* can seamlessly accept new constraints proposed by the CQI cycle without breaking existing workflows.
  - *Fail:* The architecture requires a manual teardown and rebuild whenever a new autonomous capability is introduced.
