# LiveSafe Safety Circle Completion Grant Frame

## Working Title

**Reward Readiness, Not Referral**

## Subtitle

How LiveSafe can recognize the completion of a P.A.C.E. Safety Circle without
cheapening the human emergency-contact social contract.

## Abstract

LiveSafe begins with a simple invitation: create your card, invite your four,
protect your people. The four are not leads. They are trusted humans who may be
asked to act when the subscriber cannot speak.

The Safety Circle Completion Grant recognizes a completed P.A.C.E. structure:
four distinct humans accepting four distinct roles. The reward is four months
of Plus, granted as a readiness credit. It is not paid per invite, per click, or
per account registration.

This frame protects the moral center of the product while still giving the
system a practical growth and retention mechanic.

## 1. The Tension

Growth mechanics can strengthen a network, or they can cheapen it.

LiveSafe needs growth, but P.A.C.E. is not ordinary growth. Asking another
human to become an emergency contact is a high-trust act.

The wrong frame:

```text
Get four friends to sign up.
```

The right frame:

```text
Complete your Safety Circle.
```

## 2. The Ritual

1. A subscriber creates an emergency card.
2. The subscriber names four P.A.C.E. humans.
3. Each invitee accepts, declines, or asks for a change.
4. Each accepted contact can later revoke.
5. When all four roles are accepted and notification-ready, the circle is
   complete.
6. LiveSafe grants 4 months of Plus.

## 3. Why Four Months Works

The number is symbolic and operational:

```text
Primary
Alternate
Contingent
Emergency

4 roles
4 accepted obligations
4 months of Plus
```

This creates coherence. It feels designed, not arbitrary.

## 4. The Moral Boundary

The grant must never imply:

- the invitee owes acceptance,
- the subscriber is entitled to acceptance,
- a reward matters more than willingness,
- the contact grants full medical-record access,
- responder or EXOCHAIN trust is verified,
- LiveSafe guarantees emergency outcome.

## 5. Honor Good Interpretation

The completion grant can later emit private Honor Good style receipts:

- `PaceRoleAcceptedReceipt`
- `SafetyCircleCompletedReceipt`
- `ReadinessGrantIssuedReceipt`

These receipts represent good done under consent and relationship. They are not
speculative assets, status tokens, or public clout.

## 6. Implementation Doctrine

Reward only when:

- exactly four required roles exist,
- roles are distinct,
- contacts are distinct,
- contacts accepted,
- obligations accepted,
- notification channel verified,
- contacts can decline/revoke,
- no raw data appears in metadata,
- reward is framed as readiness grant,
- product copy avoids referral-bounty language.

## 7. The Closing Line

> Four humans accepted responsibility to help one human remain protected.
> That is worth honoring.
